Apple Watch sync
GainPath is a web app and can't write to Health directly — but it can trigger a Shortcut you build once, which starts/ends a "Traditional Strength Training" workout on your Watch automatically, so you never touch the Watch by hand. Calories still come from the Watch's own sensors.
Sync workouts to Apple Watch
1. Open the Shortcuts app
It's a pre-installed Apple app — a white icon with a colorful looping ribbon. If it's not on a Home Screen, swipe down and search Spotlight for "Shortcuts".
2. Build the start shortcut
• On the My Shortcuts tab, tap the + in the top-right corner.
• Tap the shortcut's name at the top (shows "New Shortcut") and rename it to exactly match the "Start-workout Shortcut name" field above — capitalization and spacing must match, since that's how GainPath finds it.
• Tap Add Action (or the + inside the editor), type Start Workout into the search bar, and tap the "Start Workout" action to add it.
• The action now shows a workout-type field (defaults to something like "Run") — tap it, scroll or search, and pick Traditional Strength Training.
• Tap Done in the top-right to save the shortcut.
3. Build the end shortcut
• Go back to My Shortcuts and tap + again.
• Rename it to exactly match the "End-workout Shortcut name" field above.
• Tap Add Action, type End Workout into the search bar, and add it — no further configuration needed for the basic version.
• Tap Done to save.
4. Turn the toggle on above and test it
• Turn on "Sync workouts to Apple Watch", confirm the two names above match your shortcuts exactly, then tap the back arrow to save.
• Start a workout in GainPath — within a couple seconds, your Watch should show a "Traditional Strength Training" workout starting on its own (you may see a one-time iOS permission prompt the very first time; allow it).
• If nothing happens: open Shortcuts, tap the shortcut, and run it manually once — iOS sometimes needs that first manual run before it'll trust an automated one.

How GainPath works
1
Pick a day
Tap any day to start — GainPath pre-fills weights from last time you did it.
2
Customize any day
Reorder, swap, add, or delete exercises — link two together for a superset, or swipe left to delete.
3
Build your own program
Not locked into the built-in splits — name your program and add your own training days from scratch.
4
Log each set
Enter the weight and reps, tap Mark done, then rest with the built-in timer. GainPath catches new personal records automatically.
5
Calculate your plates
Tap the plate icon next to any weight to see exactly which plates to load per side.
6
Rest, with tips
The timer counts down automatically between sets, with a form tip and a preview of what's next.
7
Track your progress
Tap here anytime to see your strength climb over time.
8
Celebrate your PRs
New PRs are tracked automatically — tap here to see your full list.
9
Browse your calendar
Every session, color-coded by type — tap a day to review or edit it.
10
Reports & backups
Generate a monthly PDF report, or back up your data as JSON or CSV — right from the Export tab.
11
Make it yours
Settings is where you tell GainPath which plates/dumbbells you have, connect Apple Watch, switch dark mode, and send feedback. No account needed, ever.
