GainPath — feature backlog (fresh review)
Updated 2026-07-12 · previous review's 13 picked items shipped in v1.8.0

Shipped in v1.8.0 (2026-07-12): deload/overtraining advisor, custom
exercises, weekly muscle-group volume balance, plate & dumbbell inventory,
shareable session summary card, in-app hold timer beeps, backup health
nudges (enhanced), per-session/per-exercise notes, keep-screen-awake, quick
±weight steppers, streak shield / planned rest, CSV export, PR history
timeline — plus an added Apple Watch sync via Shortcuts (not on the
original list; requested alongside the batch).

Remaining, unpicked — same hardness ordering as before:

1. Native app wrapper (Capacitor/Tauri shell around the PWA) — HARDEST.
   Converts three permanent PWA weaknesses at once: guaranteed rest-timer
   notifications on iPhone, real haptics, and direct HealthKit/Google Fit
   read+write access. v1.8.0's Shortcuts-based Watch sync gets close to the
   write side for Apple users without this, but reading isn't as clean and
   there's still no Android equivalent — this remains the one move that
   actually lifts the ceiling.

2. Cloud backup & sync (accounts) — HARDEST. Needs a real backend, against
   the "your data stays on your device" design. Item 12 from the old list
   (now shipped, enhanced backup nudges) covers most of the practical value
   at a fraction of the cost — revisit only if GainPath outgrows personal
   use.

3. Apple Health / Google Fit integration (full read/write) — HARDEST,
   blocked without item 1. v1.8.0 ships a partial version of this for
   Apple users (Shortcuts-based start/end + optional calorie readback);
   this entry is now specifically about direct HealthKit access without a
   Shortcuts intermediary, plus Android/Google Fit (unaddressed).

4. Progress photos — HARD. Needs IndexedDB, image downscaling, and
   storage-pressure handling; must stay out of the JSON export. For a
   "definition" goal user this is arguably the missing core feature.

8. Drop sets & rest-pause sets on top of what shipped in v1.4 (drop sets
   exist; rest-pause doesn't) — HARD(ish). Rest-pause changes what "a set"
   is in a way drop sets didn't quite — short-rest continuation sets need
   their own timer/volume/PR handling.

Everything else from the previous review (custom exercises, muscle
balance, plate/dumbbell inventory, session summary card, hold timer,
backup nudges, notes, wake lock, steppers, streak shield, CSV export, PR
timeline) shipped in v1.8.0 — see CHANGELOG.md.
